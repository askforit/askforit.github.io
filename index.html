Problem

Existing delivery platforms have constraints

The existing peer to peer delivery platforms have the constraint of only delivering food and drinks from restaurants or big food chains.
We think that constraint creates a gap where other items such as a coffee or even our personal items that we forgot somewhere are also deliverable objects and that's what we bring new to the customers besides the existing food delivering from restaurants.

Solution

Askforit helps customers to get what they want without any restriction of items.
The person, called asker, puts a request on the platform and the first person to lock the task agrees with the conditions that the asker put about the value of the tip, the item to be delivered and the place to deliver it.
The main problem will be a smartphone app.

we deliver anything you want. If it's legal, we deliver!

Solve customer expectation by asking to other people to bring them their own need.

earn an extra money by making all kind of deliveries!

Earn money by working for the app and bring the expectation of the buyer.

Market


we are the first company to create a peer to peer delivering platform where anything can be asked to be delivered with a price defined only by what the asker wants to pay for the service.
There is no possible comparison with any other business what conjugated with the feature of any item can be delivered and we don't control the tip, creates a difficulty to proper define and quantify the market.

Competition


We are the first company to create an app that people can solve their all-days expectation without paying an extravagant price.
Of course, it exists company such as uber eats, Deliveroo, allo resto, ect...
But we can´t compare our app to this business as it takes much more time and deals only with professional (restaurants, bars…)

Interviews

The data that we collected through our questionnaire treated and explained:

Are you a college student?


We found it important to know if our answers were mostly answered by student colleges to be able to better understand the results.
We conclude that 76% of the answers to our survey were actually given by college students.

Do you have a smartphone?


Our idea is a mobile application to make it possible for users to make a request whenever they need it
So we had to analyze whether the users had a smartphone and everyone answered yes.

Are you forgetful of things?

Is this a recurrent problem of you?

Leave things everywhere? Or forget to buy something you need?

Through these results we realized that we could be developing an idea that would help people. If they forgot something and could not fetch it, they could make a request through our application and someone would give them what they needed.

Do you find this service useful?


It is important to know if the user finds the application useful because otherwise he will not download it.

Regarding the price, do you find for someone to get your things for you a reasonable transaction fee?


We informed people that the transaction fee would be equal to 15 cents.
We realized that 65% of people were willing to pay a fee to get someone to get their things.

Traction


Even by being biased and done with a small sample, our survey answers are conclusive that there is some interest on this kind of service.
To get some real traction we plan to start by a place where people know each other and there is enough people to create requests and answers: Católica Lisbon. To get that we want to use the powerfull word of mouth and digital ads.

Next Steps


After we test the product on catolica lisbon, if the results are promising, we plan to bet heavily on the Lisbon area to get awareness and some downloads, always with teens and young adults as the target due to the fact that normally they are the early adopters of new things and are more open to try it.
